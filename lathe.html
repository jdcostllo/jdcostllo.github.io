Blazing Fast Turning

Engineering a lathe with a clever autofeed mechanism.

This course is a notoriously challenging test of mechanical aptitude, requiring students to design and construct a desktop lathe which meets a litany of self-assigned functional requirements. For this course, I had the role of a “modeling guru,” meaning I was responsible for compiling many of the governing mathematics into scripts and spreadsheets that would inform our design decisions.

At points in the semester, I also found myself doing a large amount of project management, FEA, testing, CAD, machining, fabrication, and metrology. I was also entrusted by my teammates as the machinist in the final competition, which required each team to make a test part. Unbelievably, we won!
