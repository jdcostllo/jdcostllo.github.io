Sliding for the Perfect Shot

Using principles of power electronics to prototype a camera slide.

This work was completed as part of course requirements for MIT Course 6.222 (Formerly 6.330): Power Electronics Laboratory. Professor Steve Leeb is the instructor.

I've always been fascinated by electronics. I've leveraged that fascination as part of my work designing and constructing PLC-based controllers and data acquisition systems in the GEAR Lab. I feel I have a decent grip of industrial electronics including design, component selection, and implementation.

However, I yearned for a more foundational understanding, including the selection of integrated circuits, circuit design, and power system design. Power Electronics Laboratory gave me this insight and far, far more. This course was, beyond a shadow of doubt, the most rewarding and most challenging course I have ever taken. Now, more than ever, I feel like I have a grasp of this fascinating and rewarding field of engineering.

The course culminated in a final project. I decided to combine a few course concepts to design a prototype of a battery-powered, remote controlled, linear camera slide.
