Automated, Effective Fish Feeding

Developing and prototyping a manifold selector valve for automated waterborne fish feeding.

Taught by Amos Winter, who is also my PI in the GEAR Center, this course sought to inform engineering students of both the cultural considerations and engineering considerations required to make successful products for emerging and developing markets.

My team and I partnered with Innovasea to deliver an improved design for their “manifold selector valve,” a critical part of their automated fish aquaculture feeding system. This device, which routes waterborne food to different fish pens, was designed with low cost components including wiper motors, off-the-shelf valves, rotary potentiometers, limit switches, and relays. Compared to conventional, electrically or pneumatically actuated ball valves, our unique design required fewer actuators and was modular.

On this team, I did considerable modeling, CAD, fabrication, and I was primarily responsible for the design and validation of the PLC based control system. I was also responsible for background market research on existing aquaculture systems in developed and developing markets.
