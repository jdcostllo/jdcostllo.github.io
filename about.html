About Me

I was born and raised in a suburb of Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. In the fall of 2013, I began my undergraduate education at Boston University. Since 2013, I've called Boston and its suburbs home.

Not long after graduating, I found myself fortunate to secure a role as a technician with a mechanical engineering research group at MIT, the Global Engineering and Research (GEAR) Lab. I spent years surrounded by brilliant researchers, engineers, and scientists helping them advance their research and design field pilots. Like those brilliant researchers, I was deeply motivated by the opportunity to work on projects which sought to bring tangible, beneficial, global change to irrigation, desalination, prosthetics, and more.

While I was working as a technician, never once did I consider that I'd become a student again, let alone at MIT. Unexpectedly, and as of writing this, here I am now, approaching the completion of my master's of science in mechanical engineering at MIT in the GEAR Lab (now the GEAR Center). I couldn't be more fortunate or more grateful for this magical opportunity.

As I approach the next step in my career, I hope to continue paying forward my great fortune. I hope to continue working on world-changing technologies that break barriers, upset existing markets, and enable more people with the tools and technologies that will not only positively impact their careers but positively impact their lives as well.
